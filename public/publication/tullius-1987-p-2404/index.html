Chemical ‘snapshots’ of DNA: using the hydroxyl radical to study the structure of DNA and DNA-protein complexes

Thomas D. Tullius
January 1987
DOI

Abstract

Details of DNA structure are revealed by a new chemical method that makes use of the hydroxyl radical (·OH) to cut the backbone of any DNA molecule of interest. This tiny molecule can also be used to make very high resolution ‘footprints’ of protein-DNA complexes.
